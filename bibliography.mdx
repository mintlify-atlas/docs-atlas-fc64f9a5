The course draws on a curated set of textbooks covering geometry, deep learning, and applied computer vision. All books listed below are either freely available online or have free sample chapters.

## Core textbooks

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card
    title="Neural Networks and Deep Learning"
    icon="network-wired"
    href="https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007%2F978-3-319-94463-0"
  >
    Aggarwal, C.C. (2019). Springer. Covers feedforward networks, CNNs, RNNs, and deep learning theory. Free to download via Springer Link.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="Deep Learning"
    icon="brain"
    href="http://www.deeplearningbook.org"
  >
    Goodfellow, I., Bengio, Y., and Courville, A. (2016). MIT Press. The definitive reference for deep learning fundamentals. Freely available online.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="Computer Vision: Algorithms and Applications"
    icon="eye"
    href="http://szeliski.org/Book/"
  >
    Szeliski, R. (2010). Springer. Comprehensive coverage of image formation, feature detection, stereo, recognition, and more. Freely available from the author's website.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="Multiple View Geometry in Computer Vision"
    icon="camera"
    href="https://www.r-5.org/files/books/computers/algo-list/image-processing/vision/Richard_Hartley_Andrew_Zisserman-Multiple_View_Geometry_in_Computer_Vision-EN.pdf"
  >
    Hartley, R. and Zisserman, A. (2004). Cambridge University Press. The standard reference for projective geometry, camera models, and multi-view reconstruction.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="Computer Vision for X-ray Testing"
    icon="x-ray"
    href="https://domingomery.ing.puc.cl/publications/book/"
  >
    Mery, D. and Pieringer, C. (2021). Springer. Written by the course professor. Applies computer vision techniques to non-destructive testing and X-ray inspection.
  </Card>
  <Card
    title="Computer Vision for X-ray Testing (2015 free sample)"
    icon="file-pdf"
    href="https://www.dropbox.com/s/6ojxn5h1s0dxhd4/bok%253A978-3-319-20747-6.pdf"
  >
    Mery, D. (2015). Free sample PDF of the earlier edition. Useful companion reading for the geometry and reconstruction chapters.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Supplementary reading

- **Fairness and Machine Learning** — Barocas, Hardt, and Narayanan. Freely available at [fairmlbook.org](https://fairmlbook.org/pdf/fairmlbook.pdf). Required reading for the ethics chapter (Cap04).
- **A Tutorial on Fairness in Machine Learning** — Practical overview at [Towards Data Science](https://towardsdatascience.com/a-tutorial-on-fairness-in-machine-learning-3ff8ba1040cb).
- **MinPlus paper** — Mery, D. (CVPRW 2022). Black-box explanation in facial analysis using saliency maps. [PDF on CVPR Open Access](https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content/CVPR2022W/Biometrics/papers/Mery_True_Black-Box_Explanation_in_Facial_Analysis_CVPRW_2022_paper.pdf).
- **UNet paper** — Ronneberger et al. (2015). The original U-Net segmentation architecture. [arXiv PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1505.04597.pdf).
