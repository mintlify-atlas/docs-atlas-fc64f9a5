Welcome to the documentation site for **Visión por Computador** (IEE3714 / IIC3724), a graduate-level Computer Vision course at the Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile, taught by [Domingo Mery](https://domingomery.ing.puc.cl).

The course takes you from the mathematical foundations of image geometry through modern deep learning architectures, finishing with a critical look at ethics, fairness, and explainability in AI systems. All topics are accompanied by hands-on Python (Google Colab) and MATLAB examples.

## Getting started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review the course overview">
    Read the [course overview](/course-overview) to understand the schedule, topics, and expectations.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up your Python environment">
    Most hands-on exercises run in Google Colab — no local setup required. Open any Colab link directly from the topic pages.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start with geometry">
    Begin with [homogeneous coordinates](/geometry/homogeneous-coordinates) to build the mathematical foundation needed for the rest of the course.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Explore deep learning">
    Once comfortable with geometry, move to [deep learning](/deep-learning/introduction) to work with CNNs, YOLO, and Transformers.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Recorded lectures from the 2021 edition of the course are linked throughout the materials as supplementary viewing. Look for **Video: Clase grabada** references on each topic page.
</Note>
